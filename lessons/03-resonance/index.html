03 · Resonance & the squelch

The filter learns to sing. One new knob, one new toggle — and suddenly it sounds like acid.

idea 1Resonance gives the filter a favourite note

Push a kid on a swing at exactly the swing's own rhythm and a tiny push grows huge. Everything that can wiggle has a rhythm it prefers — that preference is resonance. Wine glasses, bridges, bathroom acoustics: all playing favourites.

Lesson 02's filter treated everything below the cutoff equally. The RESONANCE knob makes the filter boost the frequencies right at the cutoff edge before the slope cuts everything above — look at the orange curve: turning it up grows a horn at the cutoff line.

Try it: ⏻ power on → ▶ hold note → raise resonance to ~15 dB → now slowly drag the CUTOFF. That whistle gliding along the harmonics is the resonant peak sweeping across the stack — each harmonic it passes gets its moment of glory. With resonance at 0 this was just "opening a door"; now the door sings.

idea 2Ping it — a resonant filter rings like a glass

Flick a wine glass: one tap, but it answers with a tone — the tap contains every frequency at once, the glass keeps only its favourite and rings it out. ◉ ping the filter does exactly that: it fires a single one-sample click (all frequencies at once, remember the noise from P3) into the filter, no note held.

With resonance low, you hear the click. Turn resonance up and the click becomes a pitched "boing" — the filter ringing at the cutoff frequency. Drag the cutoff and ping again: the ring follows the knob. At maximum resonance the filter is on the edge of becoming an oscillator that needs no input at all — real analog filters (and lesson 04's ladder) tip over that edge and self-oscillate into a pure sine whistle. That whistle mixed into the sweep is a big part of the acid scream.

idea 312 vs 24 dB/oct — stack two filters, get the 303's focus

Lesson 02's slope was a gentle ski hill: 12 dB per octave. The 303's filter falls off twice as steeply (24 dB/oct) — and the cheapest way to get there is to just run the signal through two identical filters in a row. That's literally what the 24 dB/oct button does, and it's how we'll build the 303 voice.

Toggle between them while a note plays and watch the orange curve: same cutoff, much steeper cliff. The ear hears 24 dB as more focused, rubbery, vacuum-sealed — less high-end leaks past the cutoff, and the resonant horn stands out more against what's left. Every "liquid" acid line owes its wetness to this steepness.

// CS corner: filters in series multiply their responses, and multiplication of magnitudes = addition in dB — so 12 + 12 = 24 dB/oct. The orange curve is honest: it's the product of both stages' getFrequencyResponse(), drawn on a dB scale (straight slopes on a log-log plot). Resonance lives only on the second stage so the peak doesn't double up.

idea 4The squelch = motion + resonance

Now combine everything you own: a rich wave (lesson 00) + a moving cutoff (lesson 02) + a resonant peak riding that motion (this lesson). Press ★ make it squelch — it switches to 24 dB/oct, dials resonance to "whistly", and plays a little bass riff where every note snaps the cutoff wide open then lets it decay back down through the harmonics. That plucky, vocal eeow-eeow is the acid squelch — made of parts you now understand individually. (Press again to stop.)

Notice it's plucky and repeating, not one slow swoop — that per-note cutoff snap is a filter envelope (the ADSR from lesson 01 aimed at the cutoff instead of the volume). That's the single most important trick in acid, and Module 2 builds it properly with its own Env Mod and Decay knobs. Here it's on autopilot so you can just hear the target.

Experiments before you leave:

  • Acid whistle: resonance max, hold note, wiggle the cutoff by hand — chase the harmonics.
  • 909 kick, for free: cutoff ~60 Hz, resonance high, ◉ ping — that boom is literally how drum machines synthesize kicks (lesson 11).
  • Wind in the wires: source noise, resonance ~14, sweep — the peak turns "shhh" into a howling gale.
  • 12 vs 24 A/B: squelch running, flip the slope buttons — hear the 303's focus appear.
